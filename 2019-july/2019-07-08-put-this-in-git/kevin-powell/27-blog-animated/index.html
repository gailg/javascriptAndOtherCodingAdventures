Colors, textures and shapes go for a ride

This animated gif was created in Photoshop. I began by creating a collection of collage materials. Some textures I started with scanned images of pencil or watercolor or inks on paper. Others I created using Kyle Webster brushes. I make use of Photoshop's blend modes and adjustment layers to push variety in my collage materials. I also use Photoshop's (and Illustrator's) pattern tool.

To cut out shapes, I use photoshop masks together with the lasso tool and brushes.

Collage materials using traditional media

A long time ago, I filled up my garage with inks and acrylics and tempera paints and lots and lots of things that helped me manipulate paint into textures. I was inspired by Maxine Masterfield's two books Painting the Spirit of Nature and In Harmony with Nature. Maxine showed what can be possible by allowing ink to dry under and around things like leaves, shells, wax paper, plastic wrap, and wax.

Digital methods

When I made collage materials (pourings) traditionally, I hoarded them. It was very hard to cut into one-of-a-kind pieces. And then there were many that were almost perfect, but not, or not big enough, or the wrong color. When you scan these mistakes into Photoshop, they can be fixed or manipulated or even turned into repeatable seamless patterns. And you can use them over and over again.

I've started using Kyle Webster brushes to help me make textures from scratch. And I can combine different textures using blend modes to get increasingly complex textures. There is so much to explore and learn.

Room for experimentation

Carla Sonheim gives the following exercise: Close your eyes and cut out a shape. Now do something with the shape. Instead of paper and scissors, I used the Photoshop's lasso tool to cut out a shape and then turned it into a mask. Then I tried out the mask on different collage materials. I've always enjoyed collage because you can move around shapes and colors and textures and discover what works. Adding the freedoms that Photoshop gives opens up even more possibilities.
